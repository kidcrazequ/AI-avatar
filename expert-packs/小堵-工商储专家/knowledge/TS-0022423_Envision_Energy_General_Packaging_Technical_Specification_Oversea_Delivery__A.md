1 Purpose
This packaging guideline for components specifies the packaging process requirements
for electrical and mechanical components of Envision Energy Wind Turbine during export
project transportation. This guideline is to avoid damage and corrosion of components
caused by adverse factors such as loading and unloading, transportation cycle, high
humidity, high salt, high and low temperature changes during transportation or storage.
Through reasonable design and material use, it ensures that component materials are loaded,
unloaded, stacked, and stored throughout the entire transportation process at home and
abroad Convenient and safe storage.
2 Scope
This guideline is applicable to the packaging of components for export transportation,
including components supplied by Envison Energy’s suppliers and Envison Energy factories.
And if suppliers are providing export transportation components and packaging, they
should provide corresponding component packaging plans according to the guidance of this
document, and obtain confirmation and review from Envision Energy packaging development,
DQE, and other technical departments.

4 Terms and Definitions
4.1 Wood Packaging
Rigid packaging containers made mainly of wooden materials.
4.2 Plywood
A material formed of odd layers of veneers by gluing adjacent layers of wood grain
perpendicular to each other. Usually, the surface and inner layers are symmetrically
arranged on both sides of the central layer.
4.3 Fumigated Solid Wood/Lumber
Fumigated solid wood is a solid wood material that has been treated using fumigation
process. The solid wood fumigation process is a traditional wood treatment method that uses
high-temperature and high-pressure steam to evaporate the moisture and resin in the wood,
and at the same time, chemical agents are added to the steam to make the wood have better
anti-corrosion and insect prevention performance.
4.4 Corrugated Paper Box
Corrugated cardboard box is a type of packaging container that is made by laminating
formed core paper with box board paper to form corrugated paper, and then processed through
printing, cutting, nailing, and other processes to make paper into boxes.
4.5 Pallet
A logistics equipment made of solid plywood, plastic, or other materials, with a flat
surface, used for stacking goods.
4.6 Stencile
Used to indicate precautions during storage and transportation, such as stacking height
requirements, center of gravity position, lifting position, etc., with icons printed on the
outside of wooden and cardboard boxes.

4.7 Shipping Mark
An identification of the materials sent to project sites, which is printed on A4 paper,

4.8 ESD Package
ESD, also known as ELECTRO-STATIC DISCHARGE. ESD packaging refers to the internal
packaging of components that could be affected by static electricity. ESD packaging usually
are ESD bags, which could prevent the generation and spread of static electricity and
eliminate static electricity.

4.10 Packaging Protection Cycle
The entire logistics and storage cycle from the time of shipment to the assembly at the
project site, including the whole process of logistics and storage.
4.11 Commodity Customs Code
The commodity customs code is abbreviated as the commodity code, also known as the HS
code, which is the abbreviation of the coding coordination system. It is a system for
quantifying the tax rates applicable/refundable for the entry and exit of various products.
The basic elements for customs and commodity entry and exit management agencies in various
countries to confirm commodity categories, conduct commodity classification management,
review tariff standards, and inspect commodity quality indicators are the universal
identity certificate for import and export commodities - HS code.
4.12 Binding Strap
Binding straps are used to secure goods during transportation, movement, shipment, or
storage. Usually have a metal tensioner, which has locking properties and will not fall
off. That is safe, reliable, lightweight, easy to operate, and protect objects from damage.
It is suitable for various environments according to different hook types.

5 Packaging Principles
1) The parts supplier shall be responsible for the packaging quality throughout the
entire packaging protection cycle, including the logistics and storage of the parts
from the supplier's shipment to project site before assembly, and the quality and
safety of the parts throughout the entire cycle, including external
environment/logistics loading and unloading, inspection operations/normal
transportation impacts.
2) Suppliers should affix relevant identification and logistics documents specified by
Envision Energy in a prominent position outside the packaging and ensure that the
content is complete and correct.
3) Suppliers shall ensure that the packaging materials used comply with the environmental
protection and harmful substance protection regulations and requirements of the import
and export customs and the country where the parts are delivered.
4) Packaging should be suitable for long-distance, multi scenario loading and unloading
and storage, including land transportation, sea transportation, outdoor storage,
customs inspection, and other scenarios.
5) For hazardous materials such as batteries and flammable materials, packaging design,
verification, and supply of products should be carried out in accordance with national
regulations and standards, such as JT/T 617, UN TDG, etc;

7 Overall requirement of Package
1) Water proof requirements: The packaging must protect the parts from corrosion or
decay caused by rainwater erosion, and also ensure that the outer packaging will not
be deformed, rusted or moldy due to rainwater wetting；
2) Dust proof requirements: The packaging should wrap around the surface of the parts
to ensure that the surface and interior of the parts do not come into contact with
external dust;
3) Moisture-proof requirements: should comply with the requirements of the General
Technical Process Specification of Anticorrosion;
4) Rust prevention requirements: If components include metal or electronic parts, they
must have rust prevention measures that can meet the packaging protection cycle
requirements.
5) Packaging test: Supplier, excluding nacelle, hub, tower, blade, should conduct
following tests or calculation:
⚫ Loading test or calculation;
⚫ Transportation test, as required by Appendix 1 Packaging Transport & Bearing
Test Report.
6) Packaging should be designed that Prioritize the use of recyclable, reusable, and
degradable materials to reduce waste generation.
7) Fastening position of packaging should be reinforced to avoid fastening damage.
8) Packaging should take the stacking requirements into consideration.
9) Should be designed with enough stacking strength, when being stacked up to 2.2m, the
packaging should not deform, tip nor displace.
10) The outer packaging needs to be considered suitable for two handling methods: hoisting
and forklift, and esures that the packaging is tought enough for both kind of handling
at the same time.
11) The packaging size should be reasonable, and the outer packaging volume generally
should not exceed 120% of the volume of the parts themselves, otherwise should apply
for permission.
12) Packaging should be designed for quick removal, and ensure that no special tooling,
like crowbar etc. are needed.
13) The packaging design should comply with domestic and foreign transportation safety
and regulatory requirements to ensure the safety and legality of goods during
transportation.
14) Packagings should comply with export customs and environmental regulations:
⚫ Regulations on Inspection and Quarantine of Domestic Import and Export

8 Detailed Part Packaging Technical Requirements
8.1 Packaging design
1) Sufficient binding and fixing points should be designed on the packaging to ensure
effective fixation of the package during transportation and prevent packaging
damage.
2) When shipping components to project sites, it is forbidden to use shipper box as
out packaging, components should be pick out and repacked before shipping.
3) Fully consider the safety distance between the packaging box and internal
components to avoid the components being squeezed.
4) The protective materials that is bound and fixed to fit the parts should not fall
off or loosen during normal storage and transportation. Metal parts should not be
used for binding, or they should be wrapped separately with packaging materials
to avoid scratches; It is prohibited to use multiple layers of wrapping film as
cushion or protection.
5) use of recyclable, reusable, and degradable materials to reduce waste generation.
6) Fulfill safety requirements throughout the entire logistics and storage process,
from the supplier's shipment to preassembly (including external
environment/logistics loading and unloading, inspection operations/transportation
impacts, etc.).
7) The out packaging should be tough enough to avoid scratching and friction.
8) Wood packaging and crating: should be designed with 30~50% loading margin to cover
loading, external shock, vibration, internal bumping, and anti-corrosive
requirements.
9) Metal rack: should be designed with 50~100% loading margin to cover loading,
external shock, vibration, internal bumping, and anti-corrosive requirements.
10) Should be designed with enough stacking strength, when being stacked up to 2.2m,
the packaging should not deform, tip nor displace.
11) Except oversize components, other packaging should be designed with fully
consideration of container dimensions to maximize container loading utilization.
12) When some certain components could be supplied by multy suppliers, the packaging
should be the same, new suppliers should provide the component with former
packaging.
13) All inclusive packaging with dustproof and waterproof level
⚫ Dust proof grade≥IP4X
⚫ Waterproof grade≥IPX3
14) The packaging should be designed for 2 layers stacking up to 2m ~ 2.2m stacking

8.2 Loading and Unloading
1) Can withstand multiple hoistings and forklifts, and there should be
openings on the packaging for lifting points.
2) Forklift 4-way entry, 2 forklift voids on each side, the height of the

used binding rigging.

3) Suppliers should provide information on the allowable load and quantity of binding
points.
4) The position of binding point should be designed adequately.
