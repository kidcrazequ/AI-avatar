1 Foreword
This specification is prepared to standardize the packaging methods for various components during domestic
transportation, and to avoid damage caused by loading and unloading, transportation cycles, high temperature and
humidity during transportation and storage. This specification is formulated to achieve safe storage and
transportation of various components through reasonable design and material use.
2 Scope
This specification applies to the packaging of components purchased by Envision Energy Co., Ltd.
(hereinafter referred to as Envision Energy) from suppliers, the packaging of components produced and circulated
internally by Yuanyuan Energy, and the packaging of components shipped to project sites.
For materials and components with other special specifications, priority should be given to the requirements
of the special specifications, and aspects not covered by the special requirements should be implemented in
accordance with this document.
3 Referenced Documents
Unless otherwise specified in this Specification, all Equipment, Apparatus, Materials, Design Calculation and
Tests etc. used / provided as contract items should be based on latest standard revisions in force at contract signature,
Specifications / Guidelines or other approved Standards or equivalent Standards applicable in manufacturing
country. If Components, Equipment or Materials do not meet with following standards, then all detailed differences
between recommended standards and following standards should be specified and confirmed by Envision Energy
timely before contract becomes effective.
Listed Terms and Conditions in the following Documents / Standards / Guidelines will be considered as terms
in this specification when referenced by this specification. If the referenced document has date annotation, then all
its subsequent modifications (not including correcting contents) or revised versions will not apply to this
specification. However, discussion of whether newest versions of these files are applicable is encouraged for all
parties who have agreed in this standard. For referenced Documents / Standards / Guidelines without date annotation,
newest version will be applicable for this specification.

4 Terms And Definitions
4.1 Definitions
4.1.1 Out Packaging, Primary Packaging
Out packaging is the packaging like crate, wood box, corrugated paper box, which provides the protection
functionality; primary packaging normally is made of soft material, like plastic film, foam etc., which provides
functions such as moisture-proof, dustproof, and anti-corrosion.
4.1.2 Shipping Mark
An identification of the materials sent to project sites, which is printed on A4 paper, laminated, and pasted on
the surface of wooden box. Please refer to relevant documents for specific requirements.
4.1.3 Packaging Protection Cycle
The entire logistics and storage cycle from the time of shipment to the assembly at the project site. Usually
considered as 12 consecutive months.
4.1.4 Picking
The process to pick out certain quantity components and then pack them with other components as project
requirements.
4.2 Explanations
4.2.1 Wood box/Crate
A packaging box that uses solid wood or plywood as the main structural component, with a rectangular shape
and closed sides. The bottom usually has a structure like wood pallet for forklift transportation.
4.2.2 General Wood Box
Usually suitable for scenarios with contents less than 200kg, and wooden boxes without frame reinforcement
on each edge.
As described in GB/T 12464.
4.2.3 Assembled Wood Box
Suitable for contents weight less than 2T, made of lumber or plywood, and the side walls are assembled
together with metal/other material components.
As described in BB/T 0040.

As described in GB/T 7284 and ASTD D 6251.
4.2.5 Solid Wood Bracket
The bottom bracket made of solid wood structure is suitable for components with large weight and size, and

4.2.6 Rack
A logistic equipment made of metal, which is mainly used for handling of heavy or large items.
4.2.7 Corrugated Box
Corrugated box is a type of packaging container that is made by laminating formed core paper with box
board paper to form corrugated cardboard, which is then processed through printing, cutting, nailing, and other
processes.
4.2.8 Pallet
A logistics equipment made of solid plywood, plastic, or other materials, with a flat surface, used for
stacking goods.
4.2.9 Binding Strap
A packaging material made from nylon or other materials that is used to secure components during the
transportation, handling, or storage, usually equipped with a ratchet lock.
4.2.10 Stencile
Used to indicate precautions during storage and transportation, such as stacking height requirements, center
of gravity position, lifting position, etc., with icons printed on the outside of wooden and cardboard boxes.
4.2.11 ESD Packaging
ESD, also known as Electro STATIC DISCHARGE. ESD packaging refers to the internal packaging of
components that could be affected by static electricity. ESD packaging usually are ESD bags, which could prevent
the generation, guide and spread of static electricity, and eliminate static electricity.

6.1 Packaging Design
1) Fastening position of packaging should be reinforced to avoid fastening damage.
2) Packaging design should consider the requirements for handling; hoisting & fork lifting postition should
be reinforced in design.
3) Packaging should be designed for quick removal, and ensure that no special tooling, like crowbar etc., are
needed.
4) The center of gravity of the packaging should be balanced, both vertical and horizontal directions, to avoid
tip over and fulfill handling requirements.
5) Fully consider the safety distance between the packaging box and internal components to avoid the
components being squeezed.
6) Binding material should not fall off or loosen under non manual operation. If the packaging material on
the surface of the part is soft and adheres to the surface of the part, the binding belt cannot be equipped
with a metal buckle, or the metal buckle can be wrapped with soft material to avoid scratches.
7) The packaging material itself needs to have the ability to resist erosion and pollution, while also providing
protection for components inside.
8) Prioritize the use of recyclable, reusable, and degradable materials to reduce waste generation.

8.1 Dust And Moisture-Proof Packaging
Necessary moisture-proof measures should be taken for the inner packaging according to the features of the
parts and environmental pressure. Composite bags made of PE, PET, PP and other materials can be used, with a
water permeability rate of no more than 5g/(m2.24h) and a thickness of no less than 0.15mm. The bag opening
needs to be heat sealed. For components with high requirements for moisture-proof and anti-corrosion, it is
necessary to consider using aluminum foil composite bags or vapor phase anti rust bags.
When components contain electronic components, desiccant should be placed in a moisture-proof bag. The
amount of desiccant used should be based on environmental pressure and in accordance with the requirements of
the General Technical Process Specification of Anticorrosion.
The material of the desiccant packaging bag should be tough enough to avoid damage, and materials such as
inferior non-woven fabrics that are prone to damage cannot be used.
When the components have fine machining finished surfaces, those sufaces should be appropriately coated
with oil for corrosion prevention and wrapped in oil paper before being placed in a moisture-proof bag. The
coating of oil for anti-corrosion needs to meet the requirements of the General Technical Process Specification of
Anticorrosion.
For large components that are fixed to bottom brackets with bolts, holes should be drilled in advance on the
moisture-proof bag, and rubber gaskets should be placed on both sides of the bag, before using screws to secure
the components.

9.5.1 Designing

requirements should be fully considered.
Depending on the structure, size, and weight of the components, metal structural components should be used
to strengthen the bottom, side, end and cover panel as needed.
For the fixation of components inside the box, priority should be given to rigid components such as bolts.
When the shape of the components is irregular, the bottom is uneven, and screw holes cannot be reserved, binding
straps can be used for fixation. When designing, full consideration should be given to whether the structural
strength of fixed points and binding points, as well as the tensile strength of binding straps, meet the requirements.
Full consideration should be given to the fixing and binding methods of the box during transportation.
For components packaged in this type of wooden box, a documented packaging plan should be developed,
detailing the structure of the crate, the fixing method of the components, and the fixing method of the box. The
design calculation process should be briefly explained, including but not limited to bottom load design, lifting
design calculation, and the pressure bearing capacity of the cover plate, side plate, and end plate.
The packaging plan should be submitted for review and archiving by Envision Energy.
Any changes to the structure design and materials of the crate should be approved by Vision Energy in
advance before implementation.

When using this type of wooden box for packaging, relevant procedures should be established to control the
packaging and loading process, and the inspection and inspection after packaging and loading should be used as

17.1.2 Internal Packaging Materials
⚫ Prohibit the use of EPS due to debris;
⚫ Prohibit the use of plant materials such as straw, rice straw, hemp, etc. as fillers;
⚫ Do not suggest to use EPO material due to hard recycle;
⚫ For filler and cushion, it is prior to use corrugate liners and grids in order to minimize the use of plastic

⚫ For easy recycling, moisture proof materials should be selected according to components features and
transportation environment pressure, it is prior to use HDPE or LDPE/HDPE composite films as
moisture protection material; caution should be exercised in the use of aluminum plastic composite
films;
⚫ All the organic materials used in packaging materials should meet the requirements of ROHS standards
for heavy metal, benzene, and halogen content.
17.2 The Reuse Of Packaging Materials
Envision Energy encourages suppliers to use circular packaging, including outer and inner packaging of
components.
When using circular packaging, the overall design should be based on the expected usage scenario and
number of cycles.
The strength of reusable packaging materials, including stacking, compression resistance, and burst
resistance, should be higher than that of ordinary packaging to avoid damage during reuse.
When planning to resue packaging, a detailed plan should be submitted to Envision Energy, including but not
limited to the following:
⚫ Detailed design of packaging;
⚫ Test plan and report developed for circular packaging according to GB/4857 or ASTM D 4169 (due to
the higher strength requirements of circular packaging, it is not applicable to the requirements of
Section 16, and more comprehensive tests should be conducted according to the standards);
⚫ Detailed reusing plan, including the WI for recycling, logistics plans, cleaning plans, etc;
⚫ Test specs for reusing materials;
⚫ Procedures and process control documents;
⚫ The packaging materials used for reusing should meet the control requirements of ROHS standards for
heavy metal, benzene, and halogen content, and corresponding testing reports should be provided.
